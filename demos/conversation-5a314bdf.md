# Demo: Conversation 5a314bdf-c17a-4bb0-9201-dcf8697cb4d2

This demo uses a real product idea discussed in conversation `5a314bdf-c17a-4bb0-9201-dcf8697cb4d2`.

## User Idea

```text
Open-source this skill and build a paid online version.
First run is free, then users pay per diagnosis.
```

## Skill Output

### One-Line Verdict

Based on the current description, failure risk is roughly 75%-85% with medium confidence.

The risk is not that the skill has no value. The risk is that "open-source skill + paid online pay-per-use version" can easily fail through weak paid demand, unclear differentiation, distribution difficulty, and conflict between the free and paid versions.

If repositioned as "open-source distribution + paid decision-ready startup diagnosis reports," the risk can drop to roughly 45%-60%.

### Most Likely Failure Chain

```text
No Market Need -> Distribution Vacuum -> Competition / Commodity Trap -> Unit Economics
```

In plain language:

```text
People think the skill is interesting
-> but may not pay for it
-> the open-source version is already good enough
-> the online version has weak differentiation
-> acquisition cost exceeds pay-per-use revenue
-> the project becomes a small tool, not a durable business
```

### Risk Scorecard

- Demand: 4/5 - Users may like startup diagnosis, but willingness to pay per report is unproven.
- Product/Tech: 2/5 - Building the website and payment flow is not the hard part. Diagnosis quality and trust are the hard parts.
- Competition: 4/5 - The concept can be copied by a prompt, GPT, Notion template, or another agent skill.
- Distribution: 5/5 - The largest risk is reaching founders and getting repeated usage.
- Unit Economics: 3/5 - Pay-per-use can have good gross margin, but low pricing may be eaten by model cost, payment fees, and acquisition cost.
- Cash/Runway: 2/5 - The project can be built lean, so financial burn is manageable.
- Operations/Regulation/Team: 2/5 - Regulatory risk is low, but the product must not be framed as investment, legal, or guaranteed business advice.

### Red Flags

- The value boundary between the open-source skill and the paid web version is unclear.
- "First run free, then pay per use" may produce curiosity but weak conversion because startup diagnosis is not always a frequent habit.
- The paying customer is not yet specific enough: indie hackers, founders, investors, product teams, and innovation teams have different budgets and expectations.

### Counter-Evidence Needed

- At least 3 of 20 people who submit startup ideas agree to pay for a full diagnosis.
- The open-source repo drives real leads, not just stars.
- The paid product provides a meaningfully better experience than copying the skill locally.
- Users say the report changed their decision, not just that it was interesting.

### 7-14 Day Validation Plan

1. Create a landing page with the headline: "Diagnose how your startup idea will die before you build it."
2. Offer three tiers:
   - Free: basic failure chain
   - Low-cost: risk scorecard and red flags
   - Premium: full report, validation plan, and kill criteria
3. Manually deliver the first 10 reports instead of building full automation.
4. Publish 5 public teardown examples on founder-heavy channels.
5. Measure submit rate, paid conversion, and whether users ask for a second diagnosis.

### Kill Criteria

- Stop or pivot if 100 visitors produce fewer than 5 submitted startup ideas.
- Stop or pivot if 20 submissions produce fewer than 3 paid users.
- Stop or pivot if free report engagement is high but paid conversion is below 5%.
- Stop or pivot if users only say "interesting" but do not use the output to change a decision.
- Stop or pivot if acquisition cost is more than 3x revenue per paid report.

### Rebuild Angle

Do not position this as:

```text
Paid online version of an open-source skill.
```

Position it as:

```text
Startup Autopsy Simulator
```

or:

```text
Startup Idea Death-Risk Report Generator
```

The open-source skill should be the distribution channel. The paid product should sell a decision-ready report: failure chain, PMF risk, CAC/LTV risk, red flags, validation plan, and kill criteria.

The user is not paying to "call AI once." The user is paying to make a better build/kill/pivot decision.
